The Problem

Most roofs don't fail at the membrane. They fail at the edge.

When a perimeter detail lifts in a wind event, the membrane peels with it. What follows is total system loss — and a liability claim that lands on whoever specified the edge.

The IBC has required ES-1 testing on edge metal securement since 2003. All 50 states have adopted some version of it. Most major manufacturers — Carlisle, Firestone, GAF — won't issue a system warranty without it.

Field-bent edge metal that hasn't been pull-tested isn't just a code shortcut. It's an open exposure on every spec sheet it appears in.

ANSI/SPRI ES-1 Compliance

Three tests. One certified system.

Required by IBC §1504.5 since 2003. Required by every major membrane manufacturer for warranty issuance. Performed in-house by Metal Edge — so the production timeline doesn't bottleneck on a third party.

RE-1 ≥ 100 lbs/ft

Membrane Pull-Off

Tests perimeter attachment against a billowing membrane pulling at 45°. Mechanically attached and ballasted systems must hold ≥ 100 lbs/ft of resistance.

RE-2 Fascia Uplift

Horizontal Wind Resistance

Verifies that the fascia metal withstands horizontal wind loads on the building face — meeting or exceeding the calculated design wind pressure for the structure.

RE-3 Coping Pull-Off

Combined Uplift + Outward Force

Pull-off test on metal wall coping evaluating simultaneous upward and outward force resistance — the failure mode most often missed by shop-fabricated edges.
